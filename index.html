  • To use this app, press a to move the selection to the item to the left and d to move the selection to the right. Pressing Spacebar "clicks" the selected button.
  • The equations tested were:
    • 2 + 5 - 10 * 2

    • 42 / 3 * 9 - 1
    • 91 + 82 - 73 / 74 * 65
  • The two conditions tested were foot and back of hand
    • foot:
      • average time: 9:32
      • average mistakes: 4.2
    • back of hand:
      • average time: 4:51
      • average mistakes: 1.3


    From these results we can conclude that being able to use your arms which are naturally more dexterous than the foot, makes it significantly easier to press keys despite not being able to use your fingers. Adittionally, using your foot to press buttons caused significant fatigue in the muscles used for fine motor movements. This shows that, in designing a foot-based interface, one should limit the number of key presses required. For long term use in a foot-based interface, it may be better to integrate a timer to change buttons. Expert users may build up enough foot strength to interact with using their feet for an extended period of time.
